The Great Epics · महाकाव्य

Itihasas — The Living Histories

The two Itihasas (Ramayana and Mahabharata) are not mythology — they are itihasa: "thus indeed it happened." They encode the complete dharmic knowledge of Bharat in narrative form, making it accessible to every generation.

रामायण 🏹 Epic · Itihasa
Ramayana
रामायण · Valmiki's Original

24,000 verses. Seven Kandas (books). The story of Ram — ideal son, husband, king — from his birth in Ayodhya through exile, the abduction of Sita, the war against Ravana in Lanka, and his return to establish Ram Rajya. The foundational narrative of Indian civilisation and the standard of dharmic virtue in public and private life.

महाभारत ⚔️ Epic · Itihasa
Mahabharata
महाभारत · Vyasa's Epic

100,000+ verses — the world's longest epic. The conflict of the Kuru dynasty: 18 books covering the dice game, the exile, the Kurukshetra War, and the aftermath. Contains the Bhagavad Gita (Book 6), the Shantiparva (Book 12), Vidura Niti (Book 5), and dozens of embedded treatises on law, ethics, and governance.

गीता 🎶 Philosophy · Yoga
Bhagavad Gita
भगवद् गीता · 700 Verses

Embedded in the Mahabharata's Bhishmaparva, this 18-chapter dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna on the eve of the Kurukshetra War is the world's most widely translated philosophical text. It covers Karma Yoga (action), Jnana Yoga (knowledge), Bhakti Yoga (devotion), and the nature of the eternal self (Atman).

Sacred History · पुराण

Puranas — The Encyclopaedias of Dharma

भागवत 🌸 Purana · Mahapurana
Bhagavata Purana
भागवत पुराण · Srimad Bhagavatam

18,000 verses in 12 Cantos (Skandhas). The most devotional of the Mahapuranas, centred on the life and teaching of Krishna (Canto 10) and the lives of great devotees including Prahlada, Dhruva, and the Bhagavata kings. Canto 11 contains Krishna's final counsel before his departure from the world.

BhaktiPrahlada's CourageCosmologyKrishna's Life

विष्णु 🦅 Purana · Mahapurana
Vishnu Purana
विष्णु पुराण · Six Books

One of the oldest Mahapuranas, covering cosmogony, the Yugas and their events, the lineages of kings, and the duties of the four Ashramas (stages of life). It provides the most systematic account of the Yuga cycle and is a primary source for understanding the framework of cosmic time in Hindu thought.

Yuga CycleRoyal LineagesDharma DutiesCosmogony

शिव 🔱 Purana · Mahapurana
Shiva Purana
शिव पुराण · The Lord of Dharma

24,000 verses in seven Samhitas. The cosmology, mythology, and philosophy of Shiva — Mahakala (the Great Time), Neelakantha (who absorbed the cosmic poison to protect creation), and Nataraja (the cosmic dancer whose dance is the rhythm of creation and dissolution). Contains profound teachings on renunciation, tapas, and liberation.

Law & Wisdom · नीतिशास्त्र

Niti & Dharmashastra — The Science of Right Conduct

अर्थ 🦅 Statecraft · Law
Arthashastra
कौटिल्य अर्थशास्त्र · Chanakya

15 books, 150 chapters. The world's oldest systematic treatise on statecraft, economic policy, military strategy, criminal law, and governance. Written by Chanakya (Kautilya), minister of Emperor Chandragupta Maurya (~300 BCE). Its legal sections on courts, evidence, punishment, and protection of the weak are remarkably sophisticated.

विदुर ⚖️ Ethics · Governance
Vidura Niti
विदुर नीति · From the Mahabharata

Contained in the Udyoga Parva (Book 5) of the Mahabharata, Vidura Niti is Vidura's counsel to King Dhritarashtra — warnings against injustice, greed, and the suppression of Pandavas that were ultimately ignored. It maps the duties of rulers, ministers, and citizens with extraordinary precision and moral courage.

Just GovernanceEthics in PowerSpeaking TruthSocial Justice

मनु 📜 Dharmashastra · Law
Manusmriti
मनुस्मृति · Laws of Manu

2,685 verses. The most famous of the Dharmashastra texts, attributed to Manu. Covers the duties of the four Varnas and Ashramas, rules of inheritance and succession, criminal and civil law, the duties of kings, and judicial procedure. Must be read critically — some provisions are historically specific; others are perennially valid principles of proportionate justice.

हितो 📖 Niti · Wisdom Tales
Hitopadesha
हितोपदेश · Counsel for Good

A collection of Sanskrit fables drawing heavily on the older Panchatantra, composed by Narayana (~12th century CE). Its stories of animals and humans encode political philosophy, ethical conduct, and practical wisdom in a form accessible to any age. Widely used in traditional gurukula education across India.

उपनिषद 🕯️ Vedanta · Philosophy
Principal Upanishads
उपनिषद · Sitting Near the Teacher

The philosophical summit of the Vedas — 108 in total, with 10–12 considered "principal." The Brihadaranyaka, Chandogya, Kena, Katha, Isha, Mundaka, and Mandukya Upanishads explore the nature of Brahman (ultimate reality), Atman (self), and their identity — Tat Tvam Asi ("That Thou Art"). Foundation of all later Indian philosophy.

चाणक्य 🦁 Niti · Maxims
Chanakya Niti
चाणक्य नीति · Maxims of the Master

A collection of aphorisms attributed to Chanakya, distilling the statecraft and ethical principles of the Arthashastra into memorable maxims. Covers friendship, enemies, family, learning, wealth, time, and the duties of different roles in society. Its maxims on the qualities of a just ruler and the character of a wise counsellor remain universally applicable.

Your right is to perform your duty alone — never to its fruits.

The sceptre (law) governs all beings; the sceptre alone protects them.

Manusmriti · 7.18

धर्मो रक्षति रक्षितः

Dharma protects those who protect Dharma.

Manusmriti · 8.15

न्यायमूलं स्वराज्यं स्यात्

The foundation of self-rule is justice.

Shantiparva · Mahabharata

आत्मनः प्रतिकूलानि परेषां न समाचरेत्

Do not do unto others what is disagreeable to yourself.

Truth alone triumphs, not falsehood.

# Chatbot Response Format - Quick Reference

## 7-Section Response Structure (For Substantive Questions)

### Section 1: Understanding Your Situation
**Purpose:** Warm acknowledgment of user's feeling
**Length:** 2-3 sentences
**Tone:** Compassionate, non-judgmental

Example:
```
Your struggle with work-life balance is something many thoughtful people 
face today. The pressure to succeed while maintaining relationships and 
personal well-being creates real tension.
```

---

### Section 2: Ancient Wisdom For You
**Purpose:** Most relevant teachings from knowledge base
**Rules:** STRICT FORMATTING — Different for different sources

#### FOR BHAGAVAD GITA:
✅ **MUST DO ALL:**
- Show: "**Bhagavad Gita | Chapter X, Verse Y:**"
- Include: Sanskrit Shloka (Devanagari script)
- Include: English meaning/teaching

Example:
```
**Bhagavad Gita | Chapter 2, Verse 47:**
कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन् ।
न कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते संगोऽस्त्वकर्मणि ॥२-४७॥

You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled 
to the fruits of action. Never consider yourself the cause of the results 
of your activities, and never be attached to not doing your duty.
```

#### FOR OTHER TEXTS (Chanakya Niti, Vidura Niti, Hitopadesha):
✅ **DO THIS:**
- Show: "**[Source Name]:**"
- Include: Sanskrit Shloka ONLY

❌ **NEVER DO THIS:**
- Do NOT show chapter/verse numbers
- Do NOT show English meaning
- Do NOT show teaching explanation

Example:
```
**Chanakya Niti:**
दुष्टा भार्या शठं मित्रं भृत्यश्चोत्तरदायकः ।
ससर्पे च गृहे वासो मृत्युरेव न संशयः ॥
```

**That's it.** No additional text after Sanskrit for non-Gita sources.

---

### Section 3: What This Teaches Us
**Purpose:** Explain the teaching in modern, accessible language
**Length:** 2-4 sentences
**What to Do:** Translate ancient wisdom into contemporary understanding

Example:
```
This teaching emphasizes that we should focus entirely on doing what is 
right and fulfilling our responsibilities, without worrying about whether 
we will succeed or fail. The scripture acknowledges that outcomes are often 
beyond our control — what matters is our sincere effort and right action.
```

---

### Section 4: Applying This To Your Life
**Purpose:** Direct connection to user's specific situation
**Length:** 2-4 sentences
**Key:** Must be personal and specific to their dilemma

Example:
```
In your case, this means dedicating yourself fully to your work, your 
relationships, and your personal growth — without obsessing over career 
advancement or recognition. Focus on being excellent in your role, present 
with your family, and true to your values. The results (promotion, praise, 
or appreciation) will come naturally when they come.
```

---

### Section 5: Clear Dharmic Directive Guidance — What You Must Do Now ⭐ **ENHANCED**
**Purpose:** CRYSTAL CLEAR, ACTIONABLE dharmic guidance grounded in the specific shloka(s)
**Length:** 3-5 action steps
**Style:** Direct, authoritative, authentic to the scripture

**Format:**
```
1. **[Specific Action]** — Because [the shloka teaches this dharmic principle]. 
   You must [concrete implementation with clear steps].

2. **[Specific Action]** — The scripture directs [specific teaching]. 
   This means [clear practical action you must take].

3. **[Specific Action]** — [Continue with 1-2 more items]
```

**Example:**
```
1. **Detach from Career Obsession** — Because the Gita teaches "You have 
   right to action but not to its fruits." Redirect your mental energy from 
   constant worry about raises/promotions to excellence in daily tasks.

2. **Practice Duty-First Living** — The scripture says focus only on your duty, 
   not outcomes. This means: Each morning, identify your core duties (work 
   excellence, family care, health, growth) and commit fully without calculating 
   results.

3. **Develop Daily Discipline** — Establish morning meditation (15 min) and 
   evening reflection to strengthen your resolve and inner peace. This builds 
   the mental strength described in the Gita.

4. **Serve with Selflessness** — Give your best effort at work and home without 
   attachment to recognition. Help colleagues, support family, create value — 
   regardless of what you receive in return.

5. **Surrender Anxiety Daily** — Each night, consciously release the day's 
   results and outcomes to divine will. This practice embodies the essence of 
   dharmic living taught in Chapter 2, Verse 47.
```

**Critical Points:**
- ✅ Each step grounded in the specific shloka mentioned above
- ✅ Authentic to dharmic teaching — not generic self-help
- ✅ Direct, authoritative language
- ✅ Easy to understand and implement immediately
- ✅ Includes the dharmic principle behind each action
- ✅ 3-5 items (not 2, not 6+)

---

### Section 6: Practical Guidance
**Purpose:** Supplementary actionable suggestions for modern applicability
**Length:** 3-5 bullet points
**Style:** Practical, modern, easy to implement

Example:
```
- Keep a small journal of daily duties and outcomes—notice how detachment 
  brings peace
- Practice the "duty, not fruits" mantra when facing pressure: "I do my job 
  excellently, I accept outcomes"
- Discuss work expectations with your manager—clarify what's under your control 
  (effort) vs. what isn't (results)
- Schedule regular family time that's off-limits to work thoughts
- Join a dharma study group or find a mentor to deepen your practice
```

---

### Section 7: A Closing Blessing
**Purpose:** Spiritually uplifting closing thought
**Length:** 1-3 sentences
**Tone:** Hopeful, encouraging, tied to dharmic wisdom

Example:
```
May you find the courage to walk your dharmic path with unwavering faith, 
knowing that your duty is your responsibility and the fruits belong to divine 
will. In this freedom lies deep peace.
```

## Quick Formatting Checklist

### Bhagavad Gita Response
- [ ] Section 2 shows: "**Bhagavad Gita | Chapter X, Verse Y:**"
- [ ] Sanskrit text included in Devanagari script
- [ ] English meaning/teaching included
- [ ] Section 5 explicitly references "Chapter 2, Verse 47" (or relevant verse)

### Other Text Response
- [ ] Section 2 shows: "**[Source Name]:**"
- [ ] Sanskrit text included in Devanagari script
- [ ] NO chapter/verse numbers anywhere
- [ ] NO English meaning shown after Sanskrit

### All Responses
- [ ] All 7 sections present
- [ ] Section 5 has 3-5 concrete action steps
- [ ] Each step in Section 5 grounded in the shloka
- [ ] Tone is warm, wise, non-judgmental
- [ ] Contains actual Sanskrit from knowledge base

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **WRONG:** Showing chapter/verse for Chanakya Niti
```
**Chanakya Niti | Chapter 1, Verse 14:**  ← WRONG!
दुष्टा भार्या...
```

✅ **RIGHT:** Just source and Sanskrit
```
**Chanakya Niti:**
दुष्टा भार्या...
```

---

❌ **WRONG:** Only showing Sanskrit without meaning for Bhagavad Gita
```
**Bhagavad Gita | Chapter 2, Verse 47:**
कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते...
[No English explanation]
```

✅ **RIGHT:** Sanskrit AND English meaning
```
**Bhagavad Gita | Chapter 2, Verse 47:**
कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन् ।

You have a right to perform your prescribed duty, but you are not entitled...
```

---

❌ **WRONG:** Generic advice in Section 5
```
## Clear Dharmic Directive Guidance
1. Be positive
2. Stay motivated  ← Too vague, not grounded in scripture
3. Work hard
```

✅ **RIGHT:** Specific dharmic guidance grounded in the shloka
```
## Clear Dharmic Directive Guidance — What You Must Do Now
1. **Detach from Results** — Because the Gita teaches "You have right to 
   action but not to its fruits." Stop checking performance metrics daily;  
   instead focus only on quality and effort.
```

## Testing Your Response Format

Before deploying, test these scenarios:

### Test 1: Bhagavad Gita Query
```
User: "How should I handle fear and doubt?"
Verify: Response contains "Chapter X, Verse Y" format with Sanskrit
```

### Test 2: Chanakya Query
```
User: "How do I choose good friends?"
Verify: Response contains source name + Sanskrit ONLY (no chapter/verse)
```

### Test 3: General Life Question
```
User: "I'm struggling with my career choices"
Verify: All 7 sections present, Section 5 has 3-5 grounded action steps
```

## System Prompt Key Points (For AI Model)

The model receives explicit instructions:

```
FOR BHAGAVAD GITA:
- ALWAYS show "Chapter X, Verse Y"
- ALWAYS include Sanskrit Shloka
- ALWAYS include teaching/meaning

FOR OTHER TEXTS:
- NEVER show chapter/verse
- ONLY show source + Sanskrit
- NEVER show English meaning
```

These rules are enforced:
1. In the SYSTEM_PROMPT
2. In the knowledge base context (format_passages_for_prompt)
3. In the augmented user question to the model
